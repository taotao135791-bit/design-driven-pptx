# Promotion & Marketing

> Applicable scenarios: Brand launches, marketing campaigns, art/fashion/culture showcases, product introductions, etc.
> Style anchor: Apple Keynote / Xiaomi product launch / High-end fashion brands / Art exhibitions / Cultural promotion / Premium brand visual systems

## Design Philosophy

- **Top-tier visual impact**: Large images and bold typography, using extreme visual tension to convey brand tone, artistic quality, or cultural depth — every page should leave the audience breathless
- **Less is more**: Each page delivers only one core message, creating a premium feel through generous whitespace and minimalist typography; restraint is the highest form of expression
- **Emotion-driven**: Evoke emotional resonance through color, imagery, and copy, rather than piling up feature specs or information items
- **Brand/theme consistency**: Every page is an extension of the brand image or theme identity; color, typography, and tone are highly unified, forming a complete visual narrative
- **Bold layout**: Break conventional grids; make good use of asymmetric composition, bleed images, oversized typography, extreme whitespace, and other techniques to create an avant-garde and design-forward feel

## Information Density: Low

- Each page delivers only **one core message**, content area fill rate **40-60%**
- Generous whitespace is a deliberate design choice, not a lack of content
- Key numbers, slogans, and core concepts use oversized font on standalone pages, giving them a ceremonial weight
- The fewer the words, the heavier each word carries; copy must be meticulously refined

## Text-to-Visual Ratio: Image & Visual-Dominant

- Recommended for content pages: approximately 30% text + 10% charts + 60% images/visual elements
- Make extensive use of high-quality images: product photos, scene photos, art photography, cultural materials, fashion editorials, etc.
- Text should be refined, slogan-style expression, rejecting lengthy paragraphs
- Art/culture scenarios can use artwork images, exhibition photographs, and texture/material images to replace conventional materials

## Color Guidance

- **Color is tone**: Color is the primary language for conveying brand identity and emotional atmosphere
- Brand launches: Build around brand colors; recommend brand primary + black/white + one accent color
- Fashion/art scenarios: Bold color schemes such as high-saturation color clashing, Morandi palettes, monochromatic shade gradients are acceptable
- Cultural promotion: Extract colors from thematic cultural imagery (e.g., ink gray, Forbidden City red, Dunhuang gold)
- Subtle gradients within the brand or theme color family are allowed to enhance atmospheric depth

## Font Guidance

- **Typography is the core carrier of design sensibility**; choosing fonts with premium aesthetics is crucial
- Titles/Slogans: Sans-serif Bold or Light, extremely large sizes (40-68px), pursuing visual impact
- Art/culture scenario titles may use serif or calligraphic fonts to strengthen thematic identity
- Body text: Sans-serif Light/Regular, moderate size (18-22px; use 22px when page text content is light, 20px for moderate; must not go below 18px), maintaining elegant restraint
- Use weight contrast (Bold vs Light) instead of size contrast for a more sophisticated hierarchy
- Letter spacing and line spacing must be carefully tuned; generous spacing feels more premium than tight arrangement

## Content Pages

- **Centered slogan page**: A single sentence fills the entire screen with oversized font, creating ceremonial impact
- **Full-bleed image + overlaid text**: Image fills the entire page; text presented via semi-transparent overlay or direct overlay
- **Data KPI card page**: Big numbers + minimalist annotations; the numbers themselves are the visual protagonist
- **Left-right split/asymmetric layout**: One side large image, one side text; proportions can be boldly skewed (e.g., 70:30)
- **Solid/gradient background + single-line copy**: Minimalism taken to the extreme, carried by color and typography alone
- Each page carries only one message; no fixed title bar, no page numbers, no navigation elements
- Encourage breaking conventions: experiment with bleed layouts, diagonal splits, circular crops, and other non-standard compositions

## Narrative Style: Story & Emotion-Driven

- A brand storyline or thematic thread runs through the entire presentation, with a clear emotional rhythm (buildup, climax, afterglow)
- Copy is refined and powerful, slogan-style expression; every sentence deserves to be remembered
- Emotional language and rational data alternate; emotion moves hearts, reason builds trust
- Art/culture scenarios can incorporate poetic expression and metaphorical rhetoric to elevate textual quality
- Avoid a lecturing tone; let visuals and words allow the audience to feel for themselves

## Content Expression Techniques

- **Ambient lighting effects** (core technique): Extra-wide ellipse with radial gradient, creating stage-level light and shadow atmosphere
- **Eclipse effect**: Circular halo/mask, creating a sense of mystery and ceremony
- **KPI data cards**: Big numbers in independent groups, creating visual impact through font size and weight
- **Slogan expression**: Core copy in oversized font, centered; one sentence sets the tone
- **Arc horizon line**: Bottom arc division, adding spatial depth and elegance
- **Speaker cover**: Person image + brand information, establishing personal/brand connection
- **Bleed images**: Images extending beyond page edges, breaking frame constraints
- **Mask overlay**: Semi-transparent color blocks over images, preserving image texture while maintaining text readability
- **Texture & materials**: Subtle textures layered over solid backgrounds (paper, fabric, metal), adding tactile depth

## Decoration Prohibitions

| Prohibited | Alternative |
|--------|---------|
| Dense text paragraphs | Refine into short phrases or slogans |
| Complex data tables | Big numbers + minimalist charts |
| Low-quality/watermarked images | High-quality brand materials or art photography |
| Generic clip art | Brand-customized visual materials or high-quality icons |
| Too much information crammed on one page | Split into multiple pages, one focus per page |
| Corporate template feel/generic look | Strengthen the brand or theme's unique visual language |
| Cheap gradients/flashy effects | Restrained premium gradients or solid colors |
| Overly small font sizes and cramped layout | Large font sizes, wide spacing, ample whitespace |
| Inconsistent mixed-style elements | Highly unified visual language throughout |
| Rounded rectangles | Unless strongly tied to a specific brand visual system, prefer sharp corners or borderless layouts to maintain a minimalist premium feel |
