# Strategic Planning

> Applicable scenarios: Project proposals, business plans, fundraising pitch decks, startup competitions, investment proposals, strategic planning reports, etc.
> Style anchor: Top consulting firm strategic reports + YC/Sequoia Capital recommended pitch deck style + classic fundraising presentations from Airbnb/Uber, etc.

## Design Philosophy

- **Grand vision, macro perspective**: Start from industry trends and the big picture, building a grand narrative framework that conveys strategic height and scope to the audience
- **Clear storyline**: Use a narrative framework familiar to decision-makers (Background/Pain point → Objective → Path → Expected returns → Safeguard mechanisms) to tell a complete strategic story
- **Key points prominent**: One core argument per page; quick browsing reveals the full strategic picture
- **Data persuasiveness**: Key business data (market size, growth rate, ROI, expected returns) must be prominent and credible, with all conclusions backed by data
- **Premium feel and restraint**: Professional, steady, and grand, striking a balance between authority and modernity; avoid all cheap or flashy design elements
- **Returns-oriented**: Every planning step is explicitly linked to expected output, enabling decision-makers to clearly see the cause-and-effect relationship between investment and returns

## Information Density: Medium to High

- Content area fill rate **65-85%**, overall density higher than typical presentations
- Each page focuses on **one core argument**, but allows richer data and charts for thorough substantiation
- Core data pages (market size, financial projections, ROI analysis) lean toward high density, with at least 3 big numbers + 1 chart + 3 bullet point explanations
- Strategic vision pages and summary pages use moderate density, retaining appropriate whitespace to convey a premium feel
- Pathway planning pages and plan comparison pages allow high-density layout, structured through cards, tables, timelines, etc.
- 4-6 key information points per page; core argumentation pages can reach 7

> **Principle: After flipping through all slides in 10 minutes, decision-makers can clearly recall the strategic objectives, implementation path, and expected returns**

### Density Reference

Typical composition of medium-high density content pages (1280x720 canvas): Data page baseline requires 3 big numbers + 1 chart + 3 bullet point explanations — no less than this amount; pathway planning pages need at least a timeline/flowchart + phase objectives + key milestones; strategic vision pages need at least a title + 3-4 bullet points + supporting image or icons — a page must not end with just a title and a few lines of text.

## Text-to-Visual Ratio: Balanced, Data-Leaning

- Recommended for content pages: approximately 45% text/data + 35% charts + 20% images/icons
- Market analysis and financial pages are chart-dominant (bar charts, line charts, pie charts, combo charts); common chart types not defined in pptd.md (such as waterfall charts) can be built using shape + text box combinations
- Pathway planning pages use timelines, flowcharts, and other structured graphics
- Product pages and team pages use images (product screenshots, team photos)
- Icons supplement business model, competitive advantage, and strategic framework explanations

## Color Guidance

- **Steady, premium, powerful color schemes** that convey strategic foresight and professional authority
- Recommended primary palette: Low-saturation deep tones such as deep ocean blue, navy, dark green, paired with white, neutral gray, cream
- If brand colors exist, build the color system around brand colors while maintaining an overall steady tone
- Dark pages: Dark background + white text + theme color accents
- Light pages: White or light background + dark text + primary color for titles and emphasis
- Accent color: One contrasting color for key numbers, growth metrics, CTAs, etc.; gold or amber can convey a premium feel
- **Strictly prohibited**: High-saturation fluorescent colors, too many colors (>3 primary), clashing color combinations

## Font Guidance

- **Titles**: Sans-serif Bold (e.g., QuattrocentoSans Bold / Liter Bold), powerful, authoritative, and modern
- **Body text**: Sans-serif Regular, clear and readable, moderate line spacing
- **Big numbers**: Sans-serif Bold or Light (Light at oversized font provides more breathing room and premium feel)
- Font size hierarchy: Cover title 40-56px → Page title 28-34px → Body text 18-22px (use 22px when page text content is light, 20px for moderate, 18px for heavy; must not go below 18px) → Annotations 12-16px
- Big number display: 44-60px, paired with small-size explanatory text
- Title wording should be refined and powerful, reflecting strategic elevation

## Content Pages

- Recommended structure: **Page title (top) → Core content → Bottom annotations/page number**
- Page titles go straight to the point — they are core arguments, not chapter names (Wrong: "Market Analysis"; Right: "Global SaaS Market $200B, China Penetration Only 10%, 3-Year Window")
- Common layouts:
  - **Centered big numbers**: For market size, ROI, core KPI displays
  - **Left-right columns**: Chart + text interpretation, or current state + target comparison
  - **Three/four-column cards**: Plan comparison, competitive analysis, phase objectives
  - **Timeline/roadmap**: Strategic pathway, milestone planning
  - **Objective-Path-Returns framework**: Strategic objective at top, implementation path in middle, quantified expected returns at bottom
- Recommend using text styles to build information groupings; use rectangular cards and icons to supplement information grouping when necessary
- Key pages (market size, traction, financial projections, ROI) can use dark backgrounds for emphasis

## Narrative Style: Persuasion-Driven

- **Strategic planning narrative framework**: Background & Trends → Problems & Opportunities → Strategic Objectives → Implementation Path → Resource Requirements → Expected Returns & ROI → Risks & Safeguards → Summary & Call to Action
- **Fundraising narrative framework**: Problem → Solution → Product → Market Size → Business Model → Competitive Advantage → Traction → Team → Financial Projections → Funding Requirements
- The two frameworks can be flexibly selected or blended based on the scenario
- Each page title is a core argument, not a chapter name (Wrong: "Funding Plan"; Right: "This Round Raises $5M, Break-Even in 18 Months")
- The first half establishes demand and opportunity (why to do it); the second half demonstrates capability and returns (how to do it, what you get)
- **Expected returns must be explicitly stated**: Every strategic initiative must be clearly linked to quantified returns, enabling decision-makers to see the input-output ratio
- Language is confident, powerful, and restrained — not exaggerated but willing to take a stance; data has sources
- **Data source annotations**: When citing market data or industry reports, source notes should be appended (e.g., `Source: Institution Name, Year`), using `<a href="url">` hyperlinks to point to the original report or data page for enhanced verifiability

## Content Expression Techniques

- **TAM/SAM/SOM**: Market size visualized with nested circles, waterfall charts (not defined in pptd.md, can be built using shape + text box combinations), or big numbers + hierarchical display
- **Growth curves**: Traction data shown with line charts displaying growth trends, annotated with key milestones
- **Unit economics model**: Big numbers + formula decomposition (CAC, LTV, gross margin, etc.)
- **Competitive matrix**: Two-dimensional matrix chart positioning the competitive landscape, highlighting your advantageous position
- **Objective-Path-Returns chain**: Flowcharts or three-part structure clearly showing "What to do → How to do it → What you get"
- **ROI analysis**: Input-output ratio prominently displayed as big numbers, supplemented by cost breakdown and returns projection charts
- **Milestone roadmap**: Timeline showing key nodes, each annotated with phase objectives and expected output
- **Risk matrix**: Two-dimensional matrix (probability × impact) showing risk distribution, paired with mitigation strategies
- **Team showcase**: Photo + name + background (one line), highlighting the core team's industry experience and strategic execution capability
- **Funding plan**: Clear amount, allocation proportions (pie chart applicable), key milestone timeline
- **Client logo wall**: Logos of existing clients/partners displayed to enhance credibility

## Decoration Prohibitions

| Prohibited | Alternative |
|--------|---------|
| Long text paragraphs | Refine into bullet points and data |
| Unsupported claims without data | Attach specific numbers and sources |
| Overly flashy visual decoration | Clean, steady static display |
| Generic stock photos | Actual product screenshots or high-quality brand images |
| Information overload (>7 points per page) | Split pages; one focus per page |
| Vague competitive analysis | Specific metric comparison matrices or tables |
| Returns described only qualitatively | Quantified return figures (ROI, growth rate, cost savings) |
| Data piled without conclusions | Three-part structure: data + insight + action recommendation |
| Low-quality gradients and shadows | Solid colors or very subtle gradients to maintain premium feel |
| Rounded rectangles | Avoid unless necessary; prefer sharp-cornered rectangles or borderless layouts to convey the rigor and authority of strategic reports |
